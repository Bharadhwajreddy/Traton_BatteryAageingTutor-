📖 Glossary

Glossary

Every term here is also available as a hover tooltip wherever it appears underlined in the text.

anode potential
The graphite electrode's potential vs Li/Li+. Low SoC ⇒ high anode potential; high SoC ⇒ anode near 0 V, where SEI growth and plating accelerate.
Arrhenius
Exponential temperature scaling of reaction rates: rate ∝ exp(−Ea/RT). Rule of thumb: ~2× faster per +10 °C.
BMS
Battery Management System — monitors cells, estimates SoC/SoH, enforces safety limits, balances cells.
C-rate
Current normalised to capacity: 1C empties (or fills) the nominal capacity in one hour; 2C in 30 minutes.
calendar ageing
Degradation that happens while the cell rests — driven by temperature and storage SoC, not by current.
CEI
Cathode Electrolyte Interphase — the cathode-side counterpart of the SEI; its growth contributes to impedance rise, especially on NMC at high voltage.
cell-to-cell variation
Manufacturing and thermal-gradient spread between cells of one pack; the weakest cell limits the pack.
coulomb counting
Estimating SoC by integrating measured current over time — accurate short-term, but drifts with sensor bias and needs periodic OCV correction.
cycle ageing
Degradation caused by charge/discharge cycling — driven by DoD, C-rate, temperature, and SoC window.
depot charging
Overnight charging at the home depot, usually ≤ 1C — the gentle baseline for truck operation.
DoD
Depth of Discharge — how much of the capacity one cycle swings through (e.g. 80 % → 20 % is 60 % DoD).
DVA
Differential Voltage Analysis — plotting dV/dQ to separate LLI from LAM signatures in slow charge/discharge curves.
ECM
Equivalent Circuit Model — resistors + RC pairs reproducing terminal voltage; the workhorse of BMS and system simulation.
EFC
Equivalent Full Cycles — same as FEC: total Ah throughput / nominal Ah.
EIS
Electrochemical Impedance Spectroscopy — small AC perturbations across frequencies to separate impedance contributions.
electrolyte oxidation
Electrolyte decomposing at the cathode surface at high potentials — a high-SoC calendar ageing driver, especially for NMC.
EOL
End of Life — a defined SoH threshold (often 80 % capacity for traction use) where the pack no longer meets requirements.
FCR
Frequency Containment Reserve — fast grid frequency regulation; shallow but frequent micro-cycles.
FEC
Full Equivalent Cycles — total charge throughput divided by nominal capacity; 2 half-cycles of 50 % DoD = 1 FEC.
gradeability
A truck's ability to climb a grade at speed — a power requirement that resistance growth erodes before capacity fade ends range.
graphite staging
Lithium fills graphite in discrete ordered stages, producing voltage plateaus — which makes SoC-dependent ageing stress plateau-wise rather than smooth.
hysteresis
The OCV at a given SoC differs depending on whether you arrived by charging or discharging — pronounced in LFP, complicating SoC estimation.
ICA
Incremental Capacity Analysis — dQ/dV peaks track phase transitions; peak shifts/shrinkage diagnose ageing modes.
impedance
The cell's opposition to current flow — ohmic, charge-transfer, and diffusion contributions; grows with ageing.
knee point
The point where capacity fade suddenly accelerates (often plating- or LAM-driven) — the most dangerous feature to miss in lifetime prediction.
LAM
Loss of Active Material — electrode host structure becoming inaccessible (particle cracking, contact loss, dissolution).
LFP
LiFePO4 cathode — lower energy density but flatter voltage, better cycle life and thermal stability, ~3.2 V nominal.
lithium plating
Metallic lithium depositing on the anode surface instead of intercalating — triggered by fast charging at low temperature or high SoC.
Plating happens when the anode surface potential drops below 0 V vs Li/Li+. Part of the plated lithium reacts irreversibly (LLI), and dendritic deposits can pierce the separator — making plating both an ageing and a safety mechanism.
LLI
Loss of Lithium Inventory — cyclable lithium consumed by side reactions (SEI growth, plating), reducing capacity.
MCS
Megawatt Charging System — the heavy-duty fast-charging standard targeting ~1 MW+ charging during mandated driver breaks.
NMC
Li(Ni,Mn,Co)O2 cathode — high energy density, higher calendar/cycle stress sensitivity, ~3.7 V nominal.
OCV
Open Circuit Voltage — the equilibrium voltage at a given SoC, measured with no current flowing.
opportunity charging
High-power charging during driver breaks (e.g. MCS megawatt charging) — fast but ageing-intensive.
P2D
Pseudo-two-dimensional (Doyle–Fuller–Newman) physics-based cell model resolving solid + electrolyte transport in both electrodes.
rainflow counting
Fatigue-analysis algorithm that decomposes an irregular SoC profile into equivalent full and partial cycles — needed for regen-heavy urban duty cycles.
RUL
Remaining Useful Life — time or FEC remaining until EOL under an assumed future usage profile.
second life
Reusing packs after vehicle EOL (e.g. stationary storage) — shifts what 'end of life' means and changes the economics of degradation.
SEI
Solid Electrolyte Interphase — a passivation layer that grows on the graphite anode by consuming cyclable lithium and electrolyte.
The SEI forms in the first cycles and keeps growing slowly forever, because it never passivates perfectly. Its growth consumes lithium inventory (capacity loss) and thickens an ionic barrier (resistance rise). Growth is roughly diffusion-limited, which is where sqrt(time) behaviour comes from.
semi-empirical model
A fitted stress-factor model whose structure (Arrhenius, sqrt-t, …) is physics-motivated but whose parameters come from ageing-test regression.
SoC
State of Charge — the fraction of usable charge currently in the cell (0–100 %).
SoH
State of Health — usually remaining capacity relative to nameplate capacity; sometimes defined via resistance instead.
There is no single SoH. Capacity-based SoH (Q/Q_nominal) matters for range; resistance/power-based SoH (R0/R) matters for peak power and fast charging. A truck pack can fail its power requirement before its range requirement, or vice versa.
SPM
Single Particle Model — reduced-order physics model treating each electrode as one representative particle.
stress factor
A dimensionless multiplier expressing how one operating condition (T, SoC, DoD, C-rate) scales the degradation rate.
superposition
The assumption that total ageing = calendar part + cycle part, ignoring their interaction. Useful, common, and not exactly true.
TCO
Total Cost of Ownership — purchase + energy + maintenance + battery replacement, the metric fleet operators actually optimise.
V2G
Vehicle-to-Grid — using the truck battery to deliver grid services (peak shaving, arbitrage, frequency regulation) while parked.
Wöhler curve
Fatigue-style curve mapping cycle depth (DoD) to achievable number of cycles — borrowed from mechanical fatigue analysis.
